Strategic Support for Claim Resolution

Disputes often arise on projects, and how they are managed can greatly impact outcomes. Drawing on extensive experience with contractual claim analysis, we provide strategic support including quantification, presentation, and assistance with claim resolution.

Strategic Support

Claims Analysis & Quantification Areas

Forensic Assessment & Analysis

We specialise in identifying, quantifying and substantiating contractual claims across a broad range of dispute types. Our expertise covers:

Scope Changes

Analysis of changes to scope of work, variations, and constructive or directed changes with cost impact.

Time-Related Claims

Delays, disruption, extensions of time, loss of productivity, and acceleration impacts on project costs.

Quality Issues

Corrective work, defective contract documents, and site condition changes affecting project performance.

Contractual Breaches

Wrongful termination, suspension, breach of contract, and force majeure events.

Claims Quantification Services

We have methodical expertise quantifying costs arising from various claim types:

Cost Analysis

Cause and effect analysis, direct damages assessment, and cost overrun evaluation.

Prolongation Costs

Extended project costs, preliminaries thickening, overhead allocation and other indirect costs.

Productivity Loss

Disruption impact, cost escalation, and efficiency loss on extended project schedules.

Initial Estimates

Analysis of estimates, schedules, work sequences and progress management throughout projects.

Our Approach

Thorough & Methodical Analysis

Our approach to claims analysis is comprehensive and methodical. We begin with detailed review of contract documents, project records and all available evidence relating to the claim.

Through systematic analysis, we identify the factual basis for each claim element, quantify associated costs, and substantiate our findings with reference to contract terms, industry standards and project evidence.

This rigorous approach ensures that claims are well-founded, properly quantified, and persuasively presented to support negotiation, settlement or formal dispute resolution processes.
