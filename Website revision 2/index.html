Arbitration Services

Arbitration

Arbitration

Arbitration provides a binding method of resolving disputes outside traditional court litigation. In arbitration, parties agree to submit their dispute to a neutral third party (the arbitrator or arbitral tribunal), who considers the evidence and submissions of both sides and issues a binding and enforceable award.

Arbitration is confidential, flexible, and commonly used in construction, infrastructure, commercial, and contractual disputes.

The Construction Arbitration Centre (Asia Pacific) serves as an appointing authority for arbitrators in construction and infrastructure disputes throughout the Asia Pacific region. Where parties are unable to agree on the appointment of an arbitrator, or where their contract designates CACAP as the appointing authority, the Centre facilitates the nomination and appointment of qualified, independent, and impartial arbitrators in accordance with its appointment procedures.

The Centre maintains a panel of experienced arbitrators with expertise in construction law, engineering, quantity surveying, project management, and related technical disciplines. The appointment process includes review of qualifications, conflict checking, and assessment of availability to ensure that appointed arbitrators possess the requisite expertise and independence for each dispute.

Once an arbitrator or tribunal is appointed, the arbitration proceeds independently in accordance with the arbitration rules agreed by the parties, whether institutional rules (such as UNCITRAL, ICC, SIAC, HKIAC, or other recognised rules) or ad hoc procedures. The Centre does not administer arbitration proceedings, provide case management, coordinate communications, or participate in the conduct or substance of the arbitration. All procedural matters, hearings, and determinations are conducted directly between the parties and the appointed tribunal.

The Centre’s role is limited to the appointment function and, where applicable, determination of challenges to arbitrators, replacement of arbitrators, or resolution of disputes concerning arbitrator fees in accordance with its rules.

Key Features

  • Impartial and independent arbitrators with specialised knowledge.
  • Flexible procedures tailored to the needs of each dispute.
  • Confidential proceedings to protect sensitive commercial information.
  • Enforceable awards recognised internationally under the New York Convention.

Process

CACAP’s appointment process is designed to facilitate the selection of qualified and independent arbitrators for construction and infrastructure disputes. The process commences when parties submit a request for the appointment of an arbitrator or arbitral tribunal to the Centre. The request should include details of the dispute, the required expertise, any agreed qualifications or selection criteria, and disclosure of any potential conflicts of interest.

The Centre reviews its panel to identify candidates whose qualifications and experience match the requirements of the dispute. Conflict checks are conducted to ensure independence and impartiality. The Centre then proposes suitable candidates to the parties for their consideration and agreement, or, where authorised to act as appointing authority, directly appoints the arbitrator or tribunal.

Upon appointment, the Centre issues formal confirmation, and the arbitrator or tribunal assumes responsibility for the conduct of the arbitration. The tribunal issues its binding award directly to the parties. Arbitral awards are recognised and enforceable internationally under the New York Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards.

Why Choose CACAP for Arbitration?

  • Specialist Expertise – Access to a panel of arbitrators with demonstrated expertise in construction law, engineering, quantity surveying, project management, and related technical disciplines.
  • Independence and Impartiality – Rigorous conflict-checking processes to ensure the appointment of independent and impartial arbitrators.
  • Efficient Appointment Process – Appointments conducted in a timely and transparent manner in accordance with the Centre’s procedures.
  • Internationally Recognised – Awards enforceable internationally under the New York Convention.
  • Professional Standards – Panel members maintain the highest standards of professional conduct, independence, and ethical practice.

For further information, please contact the Centre.

Mediation Services

Mediation

Mediation

Mediation is a structured, confidential, and voluntary process in which disputing parties seek to resolve their conflict with the assistance of a neutral and independent third party, known as a mediator. The mediator facilitates dialogue, assists parties in identifying issues, exploring options, and supports the parties in reaching a mutually acceptable settlement agreement.

Mediation is non-adjudicative—the mediator does not impose a decision, determine rights, or make binding determinations. The outcome is determined by the parties themselves through negotiation and agreement.

The Construction Arbitration Centre (Asia Pacific) serves as an appointing authority for mediators in construction and infrastructure disputes throughout the Asia Pacific region. Where parties seek assistance in appointing a mediator, or where their contract designates CACAP as the appointing authority, the Centre facilitates the nomination and appointment of qualified, independent, and impartial mediators in accordance with its appointment procedures.

The Centre maintains a panel of experienced mediators with expertise in construction disputes, contract negotiation, and facilitation techniques. Once a mediator is appointed, the mediation proceeds independently in accordance with the mediation rules or procedures agreed by the parties. The Centre does not administer mediation proceedings, provide case management, coordinate scheduling or communications, or participate in the conduct or substance of the mediation.

Key Features

  • Neutral and skilled mediators with industry expertise.
  • Confidential process to protect sensitive information.
  • Flexible and cost-effective compared to litigation or arbitration.
  • Focus on maintaining commercial relationships.

Process

The process commences when parties submit a request for the appointment of a mediator to the Centre. The Centre reviews its panel to identify candidates whose qualifications and mediation experience match the requirements of the dispute. Conflict checks are conducted to ensure independence and impartiality.

Upon appointment, the mediator assumes responsibility for the conduct of the mediation. All procedural arrangements, including scheduling of joint meetings and private sessions, facilitation of discussions, and exploration of settlement options, are managed directly between the parties and the appointed mediator.

If the parties reach agreement, they prepare and execute a settlement agreement. If settlement is not reached, the mediation concludes without determination, and parties may pursue other dispute resolution options.

Why Choose CACAP for Mediation?

  • Specialist Mediators – Access to a panel with demonstrated expertise in construction disputes, contract negotiation, and facilitation of complex multi-party matters.
  • Independence and Impartiality – Rigorous conflict-checking processes.
  • Confidentiality – Mediators maintain confidentiality in accordance with professional standards.
  • Party Control – Mediation preserves party autonomy and control over outcomes.
  • Professional Standards – Panel members maintain the highest standards of professional conduct.

Expert Determination Services

Expert Determination

Expert Determination

Expert determination is a private, contract-based method of dispute resolution in which the parties appoint a neutral expert with specialised knowledge in a particular field to decide a dispute, typically of a technical or valuation-related nature. Unlike arbitration or mediation, the expert’s role is to apply their professional expertise to assess the matter and render a determination, which is often final and binding, depending on the agreement between the parties.

This process is particularly suited to disputes involving complex technical issues, such as those arising in construction, engineering, quantity surveying, programming and delay analysis, defects assessment, valuation, and contract interpretation.

The Construction Arbitration Centre (Asia Pacific) serves as an appointing authority for expert determiners in construction and infrastructure disputes throughout the Asia Pacific region. The Centre maintains a panel of experts in specialised areas including structural engineering, civil engineering, mechanical and electrical engineering, quantity surveying, project management, programming and delay analysis, defects assessment, and construction law.

Key Features

  • Access to a panel of expert determiners with specialised technical knowledge.
  • Independent and impartial appointment process, including rigorous conflict checking.
  • Determinations are typically binding and final, subject to the terms of the parties’ agreement.
  • Expert determination proceedings are confidential.
  • Efficient appointment process conducted in accordance with the Centre’s procedures.

Process

The process commences when parties submit a request for the appointment of an expert determiner to the Centre. The request should include details of the dispute, the technical or contractual issues requiring determination, the required area of expertise, any agreed qualifications, and disclosure of any potential conflicts of interest.

The Centre reviews its panel to identify candidates whose technical qualifications and professional experience match the specific requirements of the dispute. Upon appointment, the expert determiner assumes responsibility for all procedural matters, including establishing the procedure, requesting submissions and documentation, conducting investigations or site inspections, and preparing the determination.

The expert issues their determination directly to the parties. Determinations are typically binding and final, subject to the terms of the parties’ agreement.

Why Choose CACAP for Expert Determination?

  • Specialist Technical Expertise – Expertise in structural, civil, mechanical and electrical engineering, quantity surveying, project management, programming, delay analysis, defects assessment, and construction law.
  • Technical Suitability – Expert determiners selected based on specific technical qualifications relevant to the issues in dispute.
  • Binding Determinations – Providing certainty and finality for technical disputes.
  • Professional Standards – Panel members maintain the highest standards of professional conduct.

For further information, please contact the Centre.

Expert Witness Services

Expert Witness

Expert Witness

Expert witness appointments involve the provision of independent, specialised knowledge and opinion by a qualified professional to assist in the resolution of disputes, typically in arbitration, litigation, or other legal proceedings. An expert witness is engaged to analyse complex technical or professional issues, prepare written reports, and, when required, provide oral testimony to assist decision-makers such as arbitrators, judges, or tribunals in understanding the evidence.

Expert witnesses must remain objective and impartial, and their primary duty is to the tribunal or court, regardless of which party engages their services.

The Construction Arbitration Centre (Asia Pacific) provides nomination services for expert witnesses in construction and infrastructure disputes. The Centre maintains a panel of expert witnesses with professional qualifications and demonstrated experience in areas such as construction and project management, engineering, quantity surveying and cost estimation, programming, delay and disruption analysis, defects assessment, and contract administration and compliance.

Engagement of the expert witness, including terms of appointment, scope of instructions, fees, and contractual arrangements, is conducted directly between the nominating party and the expert witness. The Centre is not involved in the engagement process, the provision of instructions, or the expert’s work.

Key Features

  • Experts with professional qualifications in relevant technical fields.
  • Qualified and impartial analysis and testimony.
  • Reports compliant with rules and ADR process requirements.
  • Technical support for arbitration, litigation, and mediation.

Process

The process begins when parties submit a request specifying required expertise, qualifications, and the nature of proceedings. The Centre reviews its panel, checks for conflicts, and nominates suitable expert witnesses to the parties. Parties then engage the expert directly, with terms, fees, and instructions managed between the parties and the expert.

Once engaged, expert witnesses conduct their work independently in accordance with their professional obligations and duties to the tribunal or court.

Why Choose CACAP for Expert Witness?

  • Specialist Technical Expertise – Expert witnesses with professional qualifications in construction, engineering, quantity surveying, programming, delay analysis, and related disciplines.
  • Expert Witness Experience – Panel members with experience preparing reports and providing testimony.
  • Professional Independence – Expert witnesses committed to objectivity, impartiality, and compliance with duties to tribunals and courts.
  • Direct Engagement – Parties engage directly with nominated expert witnesses.

Dispute Boards Services

Dispute Boards

Dispute Boards

Dispute Boards involve the establishment of a standing or ad hoc panel of independent experts, known as a Dispute Board, to provide dispute resolution support during complex construction, infrastructure, or engineering projects.

A Dispute Board may be appointed at the outset of a project (standing Dispute Board) or formed when a dispute arises (ad hoc Dispute Board). The board’s role is to become familiar with the project, conduct periodic site visits, provide informal assistance in resolving issues, and, when disputes are referred to it, render recommendations or decisions. The nature of the board’s determinations—whether recommendations (Dispute Review Board), binding decisions (Dispute Adjudication Board), or a combination (Combined Dispute Board)—depends on the contractual agreement between the parties.

The Construction Arbitration Centre (Asia Pacific) provides nomination and appointment services for Dispute Board members in construction and infrastructure projects throughout the Asia Pacific region. The Centre maintains a panel of Dispute Board members with expertise in construction law, engineering, quantity surveying, project management, and contract administration.

Once a Dispute Board is appointed, the board operates independently throughout the project duration in accordance with the contract and applicable Dispute Board rules (such as FIDIC Dispute Board procedures, ICC Dispute Board Rules, or other recognised procedures).

Key Features

  • Access to a panel of Dispute Board members with demonstrated expertise in construction, engineering, quantity surveying, project management, and contract administration.
  • Independent and impartial appointment process, including rigorous conflict checking.
  • Dispute Board members with experience serving on standing and ad hoc boards.
  • Efficient appointment process conducted in accordance with the Centre’s procedures.
  • Board members committed to independence, impartiality, and compliance with applicable Dispute Board rules.

Process

The process commences when parties submit a request for the appointment of Dispute Board members. The request should include details of the project, the type of Dispute Board required (Dispute Review Board, Dispute Adjudication Board, or Combined Dispute Board), the number of members, the required expertise, and any agreed qualifications or selection criteria.

The Centre reviews its panel to identify candidates whose qualifications and Dispute Board experience match the requirements of the project. Upon appointment, the board assumes responsibility for its operations in accordance with the contract and applicable Dispute Board rules.

Why Choose CACAP for Dispute Boards?

  • Specialist Expertise – Construction law, engineering, quantity surveying, project management, and contract administration.
  • Dispute Board Experience – Panel members familiar with FIDIC, ICC, and other recognised procedures.
  • Flexibility – Appointments for DRBs, DABs, or Combined Dispute Boards.
  • Professional Standards – Board members committed to independence and compliance with professional codes of conduct.

Adjudication Services

Adjudication

Adjudication

Adjudication is a rapid dispute resolution process used in construction and infrastructure disputes, in which an independent adjudicator assesses the claims and submissions of the parties and issues a binding decision within a compressed timeframe. Adjudication is commonly provided for under statutory regimes or contractual provisions and is designed to ensure the prompt resolution of payment and performance disputes that arise during the course of construction projects.

The Construction Arbitration Centre (Asia Pacific) serves as an appointing authority for adjudicators in construction and infrastructure disputes throughout the Asia Pacific region. Where parties require the appointment of an adjudicator under statutory or contractual adjudication provisions, or where their contract designates CACAP as the appointing authority, the Centre facilitates the nomination and appointment of qualified, independent, and impartial adjudicators in accordance with its appointment procedures.

The Centre maintains a panel of adjudicators with expertise in construction law, engineering, quantity surveying, project management, and related disciplines. The appointment process includes review of qualifications, conflict checking, and assessment of availability to ensure that appointed adjudicators possess the requisite expertise and independence for each dispute.

Once an adjudicator is appointed, the adjudication proceeds independently in accordance with the applicable statutory or contractual adjudication rules. The Centre does not administer adjudication proceedings, provide case management, or participate in the conduct or substance of the adjudication.

Key Features

  • Rapid and binding determination of construction disputes.
  • Independent and impartial adjudicators with relevant construction expertise.
  • Suitable for payment disputes, performance claims, and interim determinations.
  • Decisions enforceable as a matter of contract or statute, subject to applicable law.
  • Efficient appointment process to meet compressed statutory timeframes.

Process

The process commences when a party submits a request for the appointment of an adjudicator, typically under the terms of a construction contract or pursuant to applicable statutory adjudication provisions. The Centre reviews its panel to identify candidates with the requisite expertise and conducts conflict checks.

Given the compressed timeframes associated with adjudication, the Centre aims to complete appointments promptly. Upon appointment, the adjudicator assumes responsibility for all procedural matters and issues a binding decision within the applicable timeframe.

Why Choose CACAP for Adjudication?

  • Speed – Appointments made promptly to meet statutory and contractual timeframes.
  • Specialist Expertise – Adjudicators with demonstrated expertise in construction disputes.
  • Independence and Impartiality – Rigorous conflict-checking processes.
  • Enforceability – Adjudication decisions are binding and enforceable.

Dispute Resolution Administration

Administrative Services

Dispute Resolution Administration

The Construction Arbitration Centre (Asia Pacific) provides limited administrative support services for arbitration, expert determination, and dispute resolution proceedings where parties have agreed to engage such services. These administrative services are designed to facilitate the efficient conduct of proceedings whilst maintaining strict neutrality regarding the substance and outcome of disputes.

CACAP’s administrative role is confined to procedural coordination, documentation management, and quality control of formal documents for clarity, consistency, and professional presentation. The Centre does not participate in the merits of any dispute, does not provide advice to any party regarding substantive matters, and does not perform any decision-making functions.

Key Principles

  • Neutrality: The Centre does not provide advice or express views on the merits, substance, or outcomes of disputes.
  • Independence of Decision-Makers: Arbitrators, expert determiners, and other neutrals operate fully independently of CACAP.
  • Procedural Integrity: Administrative processes are conducted in accordance with established procedural standards.
  • Limited Scope: Administrative services are provided only where parties have expressly agreed to engage such services.

Administrative Services

  1. Case Registration and Documentation Management

    • Assist parties in the submission of notices, claims, responses, and supporting documentation.
    • Verify that submissions are complete and comply with formatting and procedural requirements.
    • Maintain organised case records and facilitate orderly exchange of documents.

  2. Award and Determination Formatting Review

    • Review draft awards, determinations, and formal decisions for clarity, grammatical accuracy, formatting consistency, and professional presentation.
    • CACAP does not review, comment upon, alter, or influence the substantive content, reasoning, findings, or outcomes of any award or determination.

  3. Procedural Communication and Coordination

    • Facilitate clear communication of procedural directions, administrative requirements, and deadlines.
    • Coordinate the transmission of procedural orders and communications between the appointed neutral and the parties.

  4. Scheduling and Calendar Management

    • Coordinate scheduling of hearings, procedural conferences, site inspections, and administrative meetings.
    • Issue notices of hearing dates and maintain an organised calendar for proceedings.

  5. Financial Administration

    • Receive and hold deposits for arbitrator or expert determiner fees and administrative costs.
    • Facilitate payment of fees to appointed neutrals upon completion of proceedings.
    • Provide statements of account to parties as required.

For enquiries regarding administrative support for your dispute, please contact the Centre.

Rules and Process

The Centre’s role involves the nomination and appointment of qualified neutrals. This includes receiving appointment requests, reviewing panel qualifications, conducting conflict checks, and facilitating the appointment process in accordance with the Centre’s rules.

Our Role

About the Centre

The Construction Arbitration Centre (Asia Pacific) (CACAP) serves as an appointing authority and nomination service provider for construction and infrastructure disputes in the Asia Pacific region. Established to meet the demand for specialised dispute resolution expertise, CACAP facilitates the appointment of qualified neutrals for arbitration, mediation, expert determination, dispute boards, and related dispute resolution processes.

The Centre maintains a panel of arbitrators, mediators, expert determiners, adjudicators, and industry professionals with relevant qualifications and experience in construction and infrastructure matters. CACAP provides nomination services in accordance with its appointment procedures, ensuring the selection of independent and impartial neutrals who possess the requisite expertise for each dispute.

The Centre’s primary role is the nomination and appointment of dispute resolvers. The Centre also conducts professional training courses and confers accreditation upon practitioners who demonstrate exemplary competence. CACAP does not administer arbitrations, conduct case management, provide hearing facilities, or otherwise participate in the conduct or substance of dispute resolution proceedings. Once a neutral is appointed, the dispute resolution process proceeds independently under the appointed neutral’s direction or in accordance with the applicable rules agreed by the parties.

Our Commitments
  • Independent and Not-for-Profit: The Centre operates independently and on a not-for-profit basis.
  • Appointing Authority: The Centre nominates and appoints qualified neutrals in accordance with its procedures and rules.
  • Panel Maintenance: The Centre maintains panels of arbitrators, mediators, expert determiners, adjudicators, and industry professionals.
  • Professional Accreditation: The Centre conducts training courses and awards accreditation to practitioners who demonstrate exemplary competence. View Accreditation Scheme.
  • Transparent Process: Appointment procedures and fees are published and applied consistently.

It is reserved for those candidates who, in the assessment of the course instructors and with the approval of the President of CACAP, have demonstrated an exceptional standard of understanding, practical application, and professional aptitude during the assessed practical sessions. Each certificate bears a unique reference number and records the specific training course upon which the accreditation is founded.

Accreditation Scheme Terms

Terms governing the CACAP Accreditation Programme.

Terms of the CACAP Accreditation Scheme

  1. Intellectual Property. The CACAP designations, certificate format, and accreditation framework are the exclusive intellectual property of the Construction Arbitration Centre Asia Pacific and do not derive from or replicate the credentials of any other body.
  2. Scope of Accreditation. Accreditation is specific to the training course completed and the discipline assessed. It does not confer any qualification, certification, or professional standing attributable to any other institution.
  3. Basis of Award. Accreditation is awarded at the discretion of the President of CACAP on the recommendation of the course instructors, having regard to performance in assessed practical sessions.
  4. Panel Eligibility. Accredited practitioners are eligible for inclusion on the relevant CACAP Accredited Panel, subject to the Centre’s discretion.
  5. Use of Designation. Accredited practitioners may use their CACAP designation in professional materials. The designation must be reproduced accurately and not altered or abbreviated without the Centre’s consent.
  6. Revocation. The Centre reserves the right to revoke accreditation in circumstances of professional misconduct, misrepresentation, or conduct that brings the Centre into disrepute.
  7. Continuing Obligations. The Centre may in due course introduce continuing professional development requirements as a condition of maintaining accredited status.

These terms are effective from February 2026 and may be amended by the Centre from time to time.
