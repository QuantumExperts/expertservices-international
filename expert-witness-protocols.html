Professional Standards

Core Principles of Expert Witness Practice

Independence & Impartiality

We maintain strict professional independence from all parties and ensure our expert opinions are formed objectively, based on evidence and analysis rather than partisan interests. Our experts have no financial interest in the outcome of proceedings and always prioritize their duty to the court or tribunal.

Expertise & Qualifications

Our consultants hold formal qualifications and extensive practical experience in construction, engineering and project delivery. We clearly articulate the scope of our expertise and limitations, ensuring our opinions remain within our area of competence.

Transparency & Clarity

Our reports clearly articulate our methodology, assumptions, limitations and reasoning. We provide transparent analysis that enables courts, tribunals and legal representatives to understand our conclusions and assess their validity. We avoid jargon and present complex technical information clearly.

Duty to Tribunal

We understand and accept the overriding duty to assist the court or tribunal impartially. This duty supersedes any obligation to the party instructing us. We provide evidence that is honest, fair and assists the tribunal in arriving at accurate decisions.

Detailed Framework

Understanding Protocol Requirements

Expert Declarations & Oaths

Different jurisdictions and tribunals have specific requirements for expert declarations, oaths and affirmations. We ensure all expert statements include the required declarations, confirming our understanding of the expert's duty to the court, the requirement to provide objective evidence and our commitment to the applicable code of conduct.

Scope of Authority

Expert evidence must remain within the scope of the expert's authority and expertise. We clearly define the scope of our instructions, the areas within which we offer expert opinion and those areas where we defer to other specialists or rely on factual evidence provided by others.

Methodology & Sources

We articulate the methods and sources we rely upon in forming our opinions. This includes industry standards, published literature, practical experience, professional codes and established practices. We distinguish between matters within our direct knowledge and those based on information provided by others.

Cross-Examination Readiness

Our expert witnesses are experienced in withstanding cross-examination. We prepare thoroughly for questioning, remain composed and impartial, and are prepared to acknowledge areas of uncertainty, limitations in our analysis or aspects where we defer to other experts. We maintain professional integrity throughout the questioning process.

Concurrent Expert Procedures

We participate effectively in concurrent expert meetings and joint expert procedures common in many jurisdictions. These meetings provide opportunities to identify areas of agreement, narrow disputes and clarify technical positions. We engage professionally with other experts and remain focused on achieving fair resolution.

Continued Professional Development

Our consultants maintain current knowledge of evolving expert witness protocols, procedural rules and professional standards through continued professional development, participation in professional bodies and engagement with academic and industry literature.
